8 BAT is object-oriented and uses inheritance for code reuse. The single
9 most important hierarchy is that of `BCModel`. In most applications, a
10 user would implement a model `MyModel` by either inheriting from
11 `BCModel` directly or creating an instance of one of the @ref
12 cha-predefined-models and thus would inherit from `BCModel`
13 indirectly.

31 This structure has the benefit that `MyModel` has native access to all
32 things related to Markov chains due to `BCEngineMCMC` and one can
33 immediately optimize or integrate the posterior with the methods in
34 `BCIntegrate`. However, one has to keep in mind that multiple Markov
35 chains for the *same* instance of `MyModel` share the entire state of
36 the object. This can lead to complications when
37 `MyModel::LogLikelihood` is called concurrently; see @ref
38 cha-threading for more details.
