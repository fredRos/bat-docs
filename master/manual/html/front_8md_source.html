8 ### For the impatient
9 
10 For a quick tutorial covering the basic features, jump directly to
11 the [basic tutorial](@ref cha-basics).
12 
13 The purpose of this document is to introduce the Bayesian Analysis
14 Toolkit (BAT), a C++ package providing tools to
15 
16 * compare model predictions with data,
17 * draw conclusions on the validity of a model as a representation of the data,
18 * and to extract the values of the free parameters of a model.
19 
20 BAT was originally developed at
21  the [Max Planck institute for physics](http://mpp.mpg.de) in Munich,
22  Germany, in the context of particle physics. BAT uses [ROOT] for
23  data handling and graphical output.
24 
25 ### Structure of this document
26 
27 After the [basic tutorial](@ref cha-basics), we give a brief introduction
28 to @ref cha-Bayes and describe the core algorithm of BAT in @ref
29 cha-mcmc. Further chapters describe other tools of BAT, specific
30 statistical models, and advanced features.

33 ### Further help
34 
35 Please visit our [home page][BAThome] for an overview of papers,
36 research, and other activities related to BAT. The code development
37 takes place over at [github][BATgithub].
38 
39 This document is meant to is quickly enable to you to find your way
40 around BAT. We describe the common workflow and explain how to solve
41 some not-so-common tasks. To keep the size of this document
42 manageable, we refer to the [html reference guide](../../ref-guide/html/index.html) in which
43 all classes, methods etc. are documented.
44 
45 If you run into a problem with BAT, the preferred method is to create a new
46 issue on [github](https://github.com/bat/bat/issues). This allows other users to
47 easily find your issue online and benefit from the solution. If you provide some
48 code for us to reproduce the problem, we can help you much faster. Have a look
49 at issues with the label `troubleshooting` or `question`.
50 
51 For general questions or comments that do not belong into the public in the form
52 of issues, you can contact the developers at bat@mpp.mpg.de.
